Running a clothing brand on Shopify comes with a unique headache: **Variants**.

Unlike a store selling electronics (where 1 product = 1 SKU), a fashion store selling a single t-shirt in 5 sizes (XS-XL) and 4 colors has to manage **20 different inventory lines** for just that one item.

As you scale, spreadsheets stop working. You need software that understands the complexity of fashion retail—seasonality, returns, and size-specific demand.

Here are the best inventory management apps for Shopify apparel brands in 2025.

## 1. Fabrikatör – Best for Demand Planning
**Fabrikatör** is built specifically for brands that struggle with "What do I reorder?".

In fashion, you might sell out of Mediums while having piles of XS left. Fabrikatör analyzes your sales velocity per size and predicts exactly when you'll run out.
*   **Key Feature:** Automated replenishment suggestions based on size-specific sales data.
*   **Best for:** Growing brands that want to avoid stockouts on popular sizes.
*   **Price:** $99 - $199/month.

## 2. Stock Sync – Best for Dropshipping & Multi-Source
If you don't hold your own stock (e.g., you dropship or work with multiple suppliers), **Stock Sync** is the industry standard. It automatically syncs inventory levels from your suppliers' feeds (CSV, XML, Google Sheets) to your store.
*   **Key Feature:** Automated inventory updates from supplier feeds to prevent selling out-of-stock items.
*   **Best for:** Brands with external fulfillment or dropshipping models.
*   **Price:** Free plan available; paid starts at $5/month.

## 3. Trunk – Best for Multi-Channel
Do you sell on Shopify, Etsy, and Amazon? **Trunk** keeps your inventory identical across all of them in real-time. If you sell a dress on Etsy, Trunk instantly deducts it from your Shopify stock so you don't double-sell.
*   **Key Feature:** Real-time syncing across multiple sales channels.
*   **Best for:** Omnichannel brands selling on marketplaces.
*   **Price:** Starts at $35/month.

## 4. Inventory Planner – Best for Enterprise Forecasting
For larger brands with deep pockets and complex needs, **Inventory Planner** offers the most granular data. It's powerful but comes with a steeper learning curve and price tag.
*   **Key Feature:** Deep reporting on "Overstock" vs "Understock" to free up cash flow.
*   **Best for:** Established brands with high SKU counts and budget.
*   **Price:** Starts at $249/month.

## The "Hidden" Inventory Strategy: Reduce Returns
While these apps help you *manage* stock, the best way to simplify inventory is to **keep sold items sold**.

In 2025, smart apparel brands are pairing their inventory tools with **Virtual Try-On** apps like **Genlook**. By helping customers pick the right item visually, you reduce the number of returns flooding back into your warehouse, keeping your inventory count cleaner and your profit margins higher.

**Inventory Management Stack Recommendation:**
1.  **Manage:** Use **Fabrikatör** to plan your stock.
2.  **Visualize:** Use **Genlook** to ensure customers keep what they buy.
3.  **Sync:** Use **Stock Sync** if you have external suppliers.
